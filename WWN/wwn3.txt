          Okay.. one of the busiest weeks in the utility scene has just
       finished..  there were not many games released but shitload of fine
       utils and cd-rips.  Looks like all major Microsoft programs were
       released in the last 7 days.  The main topic of the utility scene
       this week was, surely, whether DOD release of Windows 95 was a dupe
       or not, and what is the difference between it and previous NTA and
       SCUM versions. If you wanna know the whole story, and also read
       some comments about the activity of best utility groups - keep your
       eyes open on all shit listed below.

          PWA (Pirates With Attitude) - group had a nice week..not the
       best in their career but anyway very good.  They started with
       Arcada Backup Exec Server for Netware and finished with
       LONG-AWAITED-AND-MANY-TIMES-FAKED-AND-DUPED-BEFORE Microsoft Office
       95 on 24 disks. All scene warriors were already tired of
       neverending dupes and fakes of betas and PWA release came in time.
       We all count on PWA quality in this one. Also PWA released some
       fine HP title called Open View on 5 disks in the middle of the
       week, and huge CD-RIP of Borland C/C++ 4.53. PWA is the name that
       has much less fuckups than most other util groups, but coz of lack
       of coordination or smth we saw 2 freeware releases come from them
       too. Fixpack for OS/2 is not a cool release for such well-known
       label as PWA. Hope next week will be much better for this respected
       scene veterans.

          Score for this week: 7/10

          PNC (PINNACLE) - nice to see this new group growing each day!
       More and more releases are coming from them and now we can all see
       the huge progress in their work. Sure we can still see the lack of
       organization, but such releasing power is impressive.  PNC has
       shown to everybody that Microsoft is not taken fully by other
       groups like DOD or PWA and released few small things from there.
       Also, dring the weekend we saw long-awaited IBM developers
       programming hi-end - Visual Age C++ 3.0 for OS/2!  They also had a
       few not so popular releases, like Internet Chameleon CyberSpace and
       Taskforce Commander. If this group will continue it's fast progress
       - it may soon replace some older groups at the top!  Dudes.. look
       for it carefully.

          Score for this week: 9/10

          DOD (Drink Or Die) - What can i say..you all call boards and see
       the meggage we released this week.  DOD started the week with some
       new Microsoft products, including both versions of Intellitype for
       keyboards and mouse, and very powerful accounting program from
       world-famous Intuit software (QuickBooks Pro).  Also, I can mention
       a netware tool called Pathworks 4.1 for Windows NT from the
       well-known DIGiTAL Equipment.  We continued with some unknown MS
       Access addons called Total Access Analyzer and very cool but small
       program Hurricane 1.0 (best ram doubler/accelerator/win hdd and
       video cash). The top moment of the week for DOD was the release of
       Microsoft Visual Basic 4.0 Final and Windows 95 Final Full Pack.
       Many questions appeared after Win95 release.  Here I want to clear
       all shit to prevent any misunderstanding. DOD released THE store CD
       version of Windows 95. It is not called beta or release candidate
       or gold.  Thats a final copy that people will buy on 24th of
       August. Second difference from the previous SCUM and NTA releases
       of r6 - it is not an UPGRADE! You can simply install DOD's Windows
       95 over Win95 betas, Win or DOS environment. Also you all may have
       heard the rumor about time bomb placed in Win95 r6.  Nobody knows
       is it true or not except Bill Gates and many dudes dont want to
       risk!  DOD version of Win95 HAS no time bomb at all coz it is the
       final product.  Hope you will all quit asking me about Win95 after
       this explanation.  In the very last day of the week DOD finished
       the weekend with such monsters as MetaDESK 1.1.00 Client/Server and
       OmniPage 6.0 Professional!  Hope you all enjoyed our hard work for
       you.

          Score for this week: 10/10

          NTA (Nokturnal Trading Alliance) - oldest group in scene had
       several titles but many dudes missed them coz of the lack of
       trading. I can only mention one thing called Image Plus 2.0 for
       OS/2 that I personally got. Anyway our scene grandpa Mikeysoft
       doesn't want to give up! Count on more releases from him and his
       slaves soon.

          Score for this week: 6/10

          SCUM (SCUM is SCUM :) - SCUM was kinda slow this week.. just
       several betas happened like Hyperaccess 6.0 beta 3 for OS/2.. also
       some unknown products called: Mountain Splendor and Antony Casey
       Collection - 3 disks each. Same situation remaines in SCUM for a
       long time so such inactivity is not supri- sing. We all hope that
       this group will be more alive soon.

          Score for this week: 4/10

          Thats all about biggest util groups.. here i will just let you
       know about other new hot-shots of the week. we can see PANTERA
       releases pretty often for the last few weeks. They did have some
       dupes/fakes, but this group has the potential to participate in
       competiton sometime soon! X-FORCE shows us that they are still
       alive, but the quality of their releases is not high enough to
       write a special report about.  Also wanna mention some new group
       called SCSI.. specialized in cd-rips, we lately see some kick-ass
       utilities from them, like 3-D Studio 4c1 and some NT program in
       sunday.  All other groups are not worth the time to be added to
       this report.  Work better and we will make you well-known all over
       the world! C ya in the next issue of WWN!

                                   -Jimmy Jamez [DoD President]

           New and upcoming technology on the internet includes RealAudio,
       Java, and VRML.  These new add-ons to your high profile Web Browser
       give you the ability to do special things, and help to guide the
       technology into the future.  RealAudio gives you the ability to
       listen to music from the Internet in real- time (well, almost
       real-time).  Java is a new animation system similar to HTML that
       adds on to your Browser and gives you the ability to view animation
       and scrolling while on a website.  VRML is a new virtual reality
       add-on that lets you navigate a web site using "rooms" where you
       move your mouse or cursor "into" the site, and the page renders on
       the fly to give you a virtual reality perspective.

          Real Audio can be found at HTTP://WWW.REALAUDIO.COM.  If you do
       not have the Browser add-on for Netscape or Mosaic (hopefully you
       are not using Mosaic...) then go to that URL and choose to download
       it and make sure you follow the instructions as you install it.
       Once you have it installed, there are several sites that now
       support the RealAudio system... If you have your own site and would
       like to put real audio on the site so that people can listen to
       sound while viewing your own web page, there is an
       encoder/converter on the RealAudio link that will convert your .WAV
       and .VOC files into .RA or .AU files (Files used by Real Audio).
       The links on the RealAudio site include ABC on-line, SUN, and MUSIC
       EXPRESS just to name a few.  There is more and more music added
       daily, all of it commercial.

          Real Audio works on the principle that you do not need to
       download the complete audio file to play it.  The Real Audio system
       buffers upto 15 seconds of the music file so that it can still get
       a constant streaming of information (i.e. music) while it continues
       to play. This gives the Real Audio system the ability to keep up
       with the constant flow, and there is no dropping of notes
       occurring.  This method of transferring the music over the internet
       is very effective even for PPP connections down to 1200 BPS.  Java
       is a new and improved "HTML" scripting mark-up language that gives
       you the ability to see animation on a website.  Currently there are
       no browsers available for the Windows 95 system. There are browsers
       available for 32 bit Windows NT, and Solaris, as well as Indigo,
       and UNIX V (SunOS).  If you run Solaris, Solaris X86, Indigo/NeXT,
       or Windows NT, then make sure you go to the HTTP://JAVA.SUN.COM
       site to get the Browser for your particular operating system.

          This new technology is definitely something to check out since
       most web sites will support JAVA shortly.  There will be an add-on
       for Netscape for JAVA made shortly, both for the 16 bit and 32 bit
       versions.  The reasoning there is a new Browser all together is the
       fact that it is a new markup language, not centered around HTML
       3.0, which is what Netscape 1.1 is.  VRML is the kewlest add-on for
       your Browser right now, if you can get hold of it.  There are 3
       versions, including Windows NT Netscape, Win95 Netscape, and the
       Solaris series systems.  VRML is becoming such an attraction that
       they are now marketing the add-on for your Browser, trying to make
       money off this new technology is attractive to the corporate
       generals who control the scene right now.  Since VRML is
       commercial, there is obviously a pirated version of the Browser
       add-on on the net.  Look for it if you haven't installed it
       already.  Since you can navigate the scenery "virtually", you can
       also hide doors and such to various FTP sites.  This is a major
       concern for companies doing their best to control piracy, but who
       cares.  They are there to supply us with the software we trade.

          There are new VRML sites popping up every day, although the
       biggest one: HTTP://WWW.VRML.COM has gone down for some reason.  It
       should be back up soon so that you can find the other VRML sites.
       VRML rendering comes over the net, and is based on openGL for
       Windows NT and Sun systems, and WinG for Win95.  Since this is the
       case, rendering of the graphics is very quick compared to
       conventional C++ engines (the winG and openGL are integrated within
       the operating system) and HTML engines that would be used to make
       the real time imaging required for VRML.  But, as with all
       rendering of full page graphics, this system is very processor
       intensive and can cause your computer to slow down tremendously.
       The openGL system is much more effective and is about 500% faster
       than the winG system used by Win95, but Microsoft will not
       implement openGL into Win95 at all, even in the Win95 updates.
       Rendering done on a Sparc system running Solaris is almost
       instantaneous, considering it is using Sun's openGL system and the
       Sparc is also a RISC chip.  These new technologies are great
       add-ons for your internet Web Browser.  Check them out.  If you
       need more information on these new technologies, then check out the
       URL's listed above.  If you are looking for the VRML add-on,
       contact _Warlock_ on IRC or look for it around so that you can
       install it and add it to your Netscape.

       contacts and still have the major people in the scene connected
       with us... So we really didn't lose Power as you use that term.."
