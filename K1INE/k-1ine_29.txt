	Edmonton 2600 Meeting - Location Proposal

	
Written: Friday July 12, 2002 / The Clone


The Low-Down: Since early 2002, the five year organizer of the Edmonton 2600 meetings
(Cyb0rg/asm) allowed me (The Clone) to be in charge of scheduling and the overall rule
enforcement for the meetings. Therefore I've taken the liberty of writing a proposal
regarding the Edmonton 2600 meetings. 

The Current Situation: This weekend at H2K2 in New York City Emmanuel Goldstein will
be speaking to a crowd of thousands regarding the state of the 2600 meetings. He worries
that in the past couple of years, the meetings in North America have started to "die down"
because the organizers of the local 2600 meetings aren't doing a sufficient job in keeping
them together. This has hit closer to home than the Hack Canada & 2600 people would have
ever expected. Since we moved to the Edmonton City Centre, the turnout rate for the Edmonton
meetings went from the usual 20+ people to less than 10. We feel that the reason for this is
the fact that people don't like sitting in a food court that has uncomfortable chairs, and an
non-intimate atmosphere, while having security guards watch our every move. It could be a num-
ber of things, but my theories on this are probably fairly accurate.  

The Proposal: What a long time 2600 attendee (Alan) suggested to me was that we move the loca-
tion from the Edmonton City Centre to a better location in Edmonton - just off Whyte Ave on
109st at a place called "Parkallen Pizza". I have personally been to that pizza place a few
times in the past, and I really enjoyed myself. The staff is friendly and down to earth, the
prices for food are reasonable, and the social atmosphere is great.  

If we don't act quickly: If we don't act quickly to move the location of the 2600 meetings,
I fear that they may die. I have personally made a lot of friends and exchanged a lot of useful
information over the past few years by attending 2600 meetings. It would be a sad day in Edmonton
if they suddenly ceased to exist.  

WHAT YOU CAN DO...

Give me feedback on my proposal for the new 2600 meeting location. Let me know your thoughts
on the potential location change, because this is YOUR meeting too. My e-mail address is:
theclone@hackcanada.com. I hope to hear from you soon! 

THE NEW 2600 LOCATION...

"Where is 'Parkallen Pizza' located?"
Address: 8424-109 St. Edmonton AB.
Telephone: 780-430-4777

SIT (Special Information Tones) - 6/16/2002
Written by phlux - fraud@HackTel.com


SIT's are often heard whilst scanning. They are the three escelating tones usually followed
by a recording of the sort. "Your call did not go through, please try again later"
Usually they are not very helpful. SIT's are also used by Telemarketing equipment.

ADAD's or Automatic Dialing Anouncement Devices listen for SIT's and they will parse the
dialed number from its calling database as it is not working. (this is how radio shacks
telezapper works. A clean SIT recorded to an answering machine is just as effective)
ADAD's are used by tele-marketing companies, either by stand alone hardware, or with a
computer and a modem. This is usually the setup used by various schools to nark on you
for skipping school.

SIT's can be very useful to a phone phreak.
________
Encoding;

SIT's have an encoding scheme. The duration and frequencies used in this scheme allows
for 32 different possible encodings.

Tones 1 and 2 consist of a Low or High frequency. The third tone is always 1776.7hz for 
easier decoding.

Tone 1;			Tone 2;
Low: 913.8 Hz		Low:1370.6 Hz
High: 985.2 Hz		High:1428.5 Hz

These frequencies should be +/- 1.5% stable in frequency shift and  +/- 0.2% in frequency
flutter. The duration for the first and second tone is either short (274ms) or long (380ms),
and should be stable within +/- 5% of its value. The duration of the third tone is always
long. (380ms)

The duration of silence between tones is not part of the encoding, but it should not be
greater then 4ms. Interuptions of a tone should be less then 1ms for optimal decoding.
The amplitude variation among the 3 tones should not be greater then 3dB.

____________
Announcements;

The duration of silence between the third tone of the SIT and the prerecorded message
should be no more then 300ms, and 400ms for localy recorded messages.

The amplitude of the SIT should be 2dB lower then the annoucement so hand scanning phone
phreaks do not get pissed off and try to own the system.

Announcements try to identify to the user the condition and the respective action to be
taken. Announcement types are as follows:

No Circuit (NC)
ReOrder (RO)
Vacant Code (VC)
Intercept (INT)
Inneffective Other (IO)
__
NC
A No Circuit applies when there is a failure to find a trunk or to calls affected by
Network Management Controls. The call should be tried again later.
__
RO
The ReOrder applies due to; Internal Switching Blockage, Failure to recieve wink on an
interoffice call attempt, Insufficient or mutilated digits, failure to find an available
resource, interoffice signalling link failure, all announcement trunks busy, switching
equipment congestion or protocol errors. The call should be tried again later.
(A ReOrder tone resembles a busy signal, but is twice as fast (120 interuptions per minute.))
__
VC
Vacant Code applies as per the following conditions; Vacant Code, Unauthorized CAMA '1' or
'0' plus unauthorized code (??), Nonworking 911, Out of area, terminating end office or
tandem recieves digits for nonsubtending code, no route to specified transit network, or
no route to destination.

The following announcement should tell the user that the call could not be completed as
dialed and any specific instructions for an encountered condition.
___
INT
Intercept applies when the number has been changed, vacant number, disconnected number,
non-working number, temporarily suspended service, incoming call restriction.

The announcement should specify the reason for faulty call completion and any instructions.
(this number is not in service, the new number is 313 333 3337)
__
IO
Ineffective other apply under the conditions; prefix or access code dialing irregularity,
improper initial coin deposit, screened access denial, or any dialing irregularity.

Anouncement should include instructions for proper dialing and wether or not to try the
call again.

_____________
Contemplation

This information can be used by hand scanners for more conclusive data.

It would be interesting to see a small box with the ability to decode SITs in realtime.
Computer software may be the easier solution however, perhaps a wardialer feature.

I think this information is best suited for hand scanners as many announcements are vague.
Also it may be possible to identify certain characteristics of the SIT generated at the far
end by the silent intervals. Needless to say my next scan will include decoded SIT's.

Intro:

So you have owned a DMS-300 and want to take it a step further?
Doubtful, however I am not really too sure how this information
could be used unless you have personally infiltrated the SS7.
Perhaps it may make for a good refrence. If you found this txt
useful please email me!

This file was written with the Hash Project in mind. When a working
release is made public, code lists such as these will be made into
a Hash Package and it will be part of a large telecom related database.
Learn more by going to: http://www.HackTel.com/phlux/hash


Identification of international SS7 SP's(Signalling Points) is done using a
Network Indicator NI=00 in the MTP(Message Transfer Part). Instead of our 
24bit point code system, ISPCs (International Signalling Point Codes) are 
only 14bit.


___________
ISPC Format:

 _________________________________________________
|  N  M  L  | K  J  I  H  G  F  E  D  |  C  B  A  |
|   3bits   |          8bits          |           |
|<----------------------------------->|<--------->|
| Signalling Area Network Code (SANC) |   SP ID*  |
|<----------------------------------->|<--------->|
| International Signalling Point Code (ISPC)      |
|<----------------------------------------------->|

*SP ID= Signalling Point Identification

The first three most significant bits (NML) have a range of 0 to 7
The following eight bits (K-D) have a range of 000 to 255
The fields(bits) NML and K-D represent the SANC.

The three least significant bits (CBA) with a range of 0 to 7 identifies
an SP when combined with the SANC. This is an ISPC. (ex, 3-133-7)

The national SS7 PC Admin does not administer international PCs.
If you were interested in assigning the 3-133-7 ISPC to your beowulf
cluster, you would contact the director of the TSB;

International Telecommunication Union (ITU)

Electronic Serial Numbers(ESNs) can be often be found on a sticker behind the battery on your
mobile. Many phones also have codes that can be key'ed into the handset to display the ESN
(along with other useful information) if the ESN is not already made public in the phones
firmware.  ESNs can be found in the air, and decoded. They are easy to social engineer out
of most anyone, or you can always ask your carrier if you have troubles locating yours.

ESNs are a 32bit identifier, like a fingerprint for your phone. The name should be self 
explanatory. However a Manufacturers Code is embedded into the first 8bits of the ESN,
while the remaining 24bits houses the serial number. An ESN does not further identify
a handset or any supported service associated with it.

As of August '97 the Wireless Telecommunications Bureau is not in control of manufacture
code assignments, however www.fcc.gov still has the old list in decimal form.

utalize the wireless system. For instance, without a home SID programmed into your phone 
essentially you can't access _your_ (home) cellsite, and you may have to roam with another
carrier most likely being forced to pay ungodly rates. SIDs identify a certain system area
just as the name says.

I would hope in this day and age a SID would be common knowledge for carriers in your area
as they are not confidential, one should be able to phone their local wireless carrier and
obtain the necessary SIDs. For extra fun, I have also found software to track SIDs as your
mobile hands off from one system to another, and report other information aswell.

[_What is an IRM?_]


'IRM; International Roaming MIN'

An IRM is a four digit prefix designated by IFAST 
(International Forum on AMPS Standards Technology)
that attempts to assist international roaming by minimizing
conflicts with North American MIN standards which are usually
based on their 10 digit directory number. 

[_IRM Format_]

+ The format of an IRM is as follows: 0-XXX+6D or 1-XXX-6D

+ This format again is designated by IFAST constrained to 
  having the first digit a "0" or "1". 

+ The last 6 digits of an IRM are allocated by the carrier.

--

[_MIN / MIN-Codes_]

(Available in PDF format: www.edmc.net/~theclone/NETTWERKED_ALT/IRM.PDF)


In the past a MIN is a 10 digit number that has been programmed as
the Mobile Directory Number (MDN). As a result, conflicts with MINS
in various countries hiders international AMPS based roaming standards.

At the present time there are no centralized administration for MINS
and their formats. By listing all the known MINs assignments we can
get a better look at how MINs and roaming in the AMPS locations work.

Common Language Location Identifiers

A CLLI is more/less like a serial number in the telecom industry. It can identify 
anything, but is most commonly seen in identifying switches, POIs(point of 
interface) and other physical aspects of telecom.  You may need a CLLI if your 
social engineering a switch man. It should be pronounced as 'silly'. You
may also find a CLLI while trashing, and so a list of exchanges and theyre 
respictive CLLI may prove to be useful.

The CLLI code design was developed in 1966 believe it or not.
There are many places a CLLI can pop up and as such it it helpful to be acquianted
with them.

Right now CLLI.txt only contains CLLIs for Alberta 403/780-NXXs but I hope to 
have this txt updated with a listing for all of Canada. Contribute if you can!

Now they are up to date as of January 25th, 1999 according to Telus, so not 
_every_ switch/NXX in Alberta is here ofcourse, but the meat and potatoes are.

These codes will be compiled into a HASH package compatible with the HASH client
that is in the works, see; http://www.HackTel.com/phlux/hash

Some things to note are the switch name;
205 CALGARY CLGRAB21DS1 CLGR MAIN DS1

CLGR MAIN DS1-- Calgary Main DS1
Now i don't know what CGX stands for, but DSX is where the switch may be 
accompanied by an SS7 link, however this is just speculation.
