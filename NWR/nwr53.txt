-----------[ Blitz's Slightly Biased Gaming Report]-----------
            



THE BLITZ REPORT FOR THE WEEK OF 06/29/99 - 07/06/99
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

 Well, it had to happen sooner or later. Last week the game scene saw a lot of 
the big players go down in one of the worst series of busts in the scene's 
history. The leaders of the three USA based groups were taken out of comission, 
and rumours of more on the way are rampant. Apparently there is a rat in the 
chicken coop. All this is rumour and hearsay, but from what I understand, a 
promient PDM member turned in everyone in exchange for lighter treatment. Nice. 
Hopefully he'll get his due, I believe in Karma. 

 Life goes on, however... and even though each group seems to have lost some 
of their best suppliers, the games still get released. In a way the scene is 
a lot cleaner now. Most of those who went down sought to make profit from the 
scene. Although there are still people who sell their cracker and supplier's 
work, there are a few less of them. 
 This week we see some very good games released. Kingpin and Hidden and 
Dangerous were both long-awaited games, and both got ripped.

 Class didn't land either Kingpin OR H&D, but they still managed to edge out 
PDM for the week's top group. This is mostly due to their fix of the PDM game 
Kingpin. I was a little sceptical about this one, but after playing multiplayer, 
I did see the problem. It's a legit problem, but understandable. Had PDM 
bothered to fix it instead of ignoring it I probably wouldn't have penalized 
them, but on a major game such as this the rip has to be good. People are 
going to be playing this one for a while, and they deserve a good rip. CLS 
didn't really land any spectacular titles, bringing only Skydive and Mike 
Stewart. Both are fairly minor titles, and scored as such. Trophy Bass 3D 
had the 3D ripped. Now this is all good on major titles, but you know. I 
don't want to see what is in effect a 200mb rip of a Bass fishing game. No 
thanks guys, use your heads please. CLS did a lot of stuff that didn't get 
points. The trainers were for minor games, and movie addons never get points. 
They did put out the music for skydive, which does actually make the game a 
lot more enjoyable. Other than that, they managed but one patch and the kingpin 
fix. That rounds out the week. Solid releasing from Class.

 This week, I've gone to my own group for the NWR game interview, talking 
to Toast from Razor 1911. One of the oldest members of the scene's oldest 
group, he gives insight on the recent busts and a reply to Sundancer's PPGR 
tirade.

<NWR> Hello Toast, and thank you for agreeing to do the NWR game report 
interview this week. Let's start off by getting your full nick and your 
past and current affils.

<Toast> Full Nick: Toast. AKA: Burnt Bread (sometimes).  
affils: 9000c/narc/exile/tra/mennen/scum/razor/risc/prophecy/cartel/gnx/dod/
amnesia/trg/apoc/dimension/ (chronological order too) , and umm current: 
Razor 1911, Amnesia, Trg.

<NWR> This hasn't been the best month por so for the game scene. First we 
see the French busts, and now comes word of problems hitting closer to home. 
Do you see this as a new action against our hobby, or was it just "time" to 
shake us up again?

<Toast> Well honestly i think it's a bit of both.  It's been over 2 years 
since the last large US-based bust (the ones that hit the hardest) and it 
has been anticipated for  quite a while.  But it IS also an action against 
our hobby.  The internet is STILL bringing large numbers of people to the 
warez scene, and with the advent of easily reachable warez news pages, 
message boards, other means to get information, companies are also more 
aware of what we do and how we do it. This is what  has helped put more 
money into the anti-piracy efforts and led to what we have seen here. 
(which is  unprecendented, BTW, most busts have been boards or sites, 
now: it seems tactics have changed)

<NWR> I think it's safe to say that all games will be ripped and cracked, 
regardless of these busts. What then do you feel is the government's 
motivation for going after pirates?

<Toast> Well as you can see, it's not just the government, it's also the 
companies themselves who are feeding money into anti-piracy (this should 
be obvious). They are after the people responsable for getting it out, 
and those are the major game groups and/or other release groups (read 
this util groups: you are not exempt, don't count yourself as perfectly 
safe). But really, part of busts is propoganda. Companies want their 
shareholders to know they are against piracy and for profits. Governments 
and politicians want the public to know that cybercrime is also being 
fought with, and  thus they can pick on an easy snag, the software pirate. 
Further, with joe-ibuygames reading news pages and seeing latest news on 
a bust, he willl incur on his brain subcouncious links where piracy=bad 
and thus when he goes to search yahoo for warez, he'll decide "i can't do 
this, it'll get busted!" (subnote: i tnink everyone has thought at some 
point or another, that copying a game or warezing one game can get you 
in jail)

<NWR> How do you see the forced departure of so many long-time standouts 
affecting the big picture? DVN pulled down second place on the week charts 
this week, could that be related?

<Toast> Well it is real sad. Many of those affected are legends to even 
ME, and it will, no doubt, change the face of our scene. As for divine, 
i suggest you let the big picture  show itself instead of jumping to a 
conclusion so quick. It's too early to tell.

<NWR> A lot of people don't seem to give you much credit when it comes 
to your supplying skills, but they are forgetting titles such as starcraft. 
What other big games have you landed for Razor in your many years with 
your group?

<Toast> Well perhaps they are correct? I've only supplied: Hexen (c) 
Raven/ID (disk version), Starcraft (c) Blizzard, and Co Supplierd with 
Big Boss on Star Wars: Episode One, and Star Wars EP1: Pod Racer.   

<Toast> remember disk based games? heh. 7 floppies.

<NWR> Very fondly =)

<Toast> sigh. =)

<NWR> Razor has always been competitive on the early releases, but it seems 
that your store team lacks the same polish as those of PDM and CLS. Is Razor 
going to be able to compete on the store titles again any time soon?

<Toast> Of course.

<NWR> All three of the old groups now seem to have voids at the top, with 
the possible exception of CLS, who have just recently named P????? overlord. 
Is Razor going to have a new leader, or will the gruop be run by commitee?

<Toast> As beowulf said in PPGR,  Razor 1911 is a brotherhood. It's more 
than just a leader and it's peons. Our fearless 'leader' did retire a while 
ago if you remember correctly, so has  there ever been a void at the top? 
who knows.

<NWR> Well that about wraps it up. I'd like to thank you Toast for doing 
this interview, and wish you the best in the future. Any final words or greets 
for our readers?

<Toast> A bit of a response to sun dancer in PPGR...

<Toast> Of course i can't run a group, i'm not the leader of a group.  I'm 
just a dude who's had over *4* CONSECUTIVE years in RAZOR 1911, and with 
these experiences i bring a unique amount and variance of skills and knowledge 
to the Brotherhood.  Yes, i've seen a lot of things happen in  Razor, perhaps 
you missed the RazorCD split off in '96? What about the split in early '95? i 
WAS there, just  a very shy newbie courier.  Regardless, i dont' jump from ship 
to ship, as you won't gain much from doing that. I'm part of the Brotherhood, 
and again over 4+ years of which speaks for itself. Those who have been here 
most of those years surely appreciate what i've done  As well as i appreciate 
what they, and every other single member (no matter how small) has done, even 
those who aren't in Razor 1911 anymore.  

<Toast> In Summary: I sure can't run a group, but  in the time i've been in 
Razor 1911: You quit Razor, dissapeared for a year or two, Came back to Razor, 
Left for Paradigm, Left PDM for Class, and Retired shortly after.  Next time 
your in a group for a few years, you may appreciate or undrestand what people 
like myself do. (i'm not alone BTW) =)~

<Toast> Greets: To all those who were recently hit, you know who you are. 
We are here for you guys, and wish you the best of luck in whatever your 
future has in store for you.. Keep in touch with us, and rest assured, we 
will carry on, we always do.

=---------[ VCD Group and Movie Reviews ]----------=

VCD Group and Movie Review

We would like to begin by explaining some of the common terms used in VCD 
releasing. These formats are:

CAM - A cam is usually a handheld camera snuck into a full theatre. The base 
is shaky, the audio is poor, you can hear people laughing and talking. A cam 
is distinguishable  by usually  having a combination of all three or more of 
these flaws. Most sites nuke cams for their general poor quality.

Workprint - An early release of a movie.  These are usually stolen  or leaked 
copies of movies that have made their way onto  VCD from VHS or a  variety of 
other sources. These will usually contain running time codes as well as other 
identifying  marks used in  movie production.  The quality on  these can vary 
from bad to very good depending on the quality of the source material used to 
encode the VCD.  Note: This category is  also used for very  bad screeners or 
copies of movies that just didn't fit into any of the other categories due to
defects or problems with the quality of the VCD's audio/video image.

Telesync - Basically a very good Cam. A steady base such as a  tripod is used, 
the  theatre is empty or  very close to empty, and the audio and video are up 
to par. Although, you can usually detect some  flaw with a TS.  You may see a 
random head pop up, the screen edges are noticable, or the audio isn't always 
crystal clear. While not perfect, the quality in general of a telesync ranges 
from good to  very good and is  usually the first  type of VCD  made of a new 
movie.

Screeners - These are the top quality VCDs. They can be in  either widescreen 
(letterbox) or in full-screen (pan and scan) with excellent audio  and video. 
These are made from video distributors demo tapes,  reviewers copies, insider 
copies of the film or from any number of sources. Most screeners will contain 
"1-800-NO-COPIES" or "Property of.." messages somewhere in the film, but it's 
not a  requirement  to fit  into the  screener  category.  These most closely 
resemble a VHS or LaserDisc copy of the movie. They're easily identifiable by 
their top quality.

4.) Bounces
    More and more people are getting ADSL or cable modem connections, and the nice 
    thing about these wonderful services are the static IP addresses that they afford you.
    Now instead of logging into a site, mget'ing shit to you shell, logging into your
    shell, and downloading it to your home, you can now just FTP right to the site, and
    grab your ISO, or whatever.. Saves a hell of a lot of time and effort.
    But its bullshit.
    Consider your IP address as being as important as your home phone # and house address.
    The wonderful thing about static IP addresses on the @home/adsl network is that
    there is one central office located somewhere that knows who the subscriber is
    that owns xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx IP address, and where he lives. As an additional bonus,
    if you are an @home network subscriber, you will be have the wonderful "feature"
    of having everything you download cached..
    IE: if you are downloading Backstreet.Boys.mp3, they will know, cause they are caching
    it!!! ... and your IP will be flagged as downloading "illegal" mp3's... Hooray!!!
    Lucky you!
    Can anyone see how important having a bounce suddenly becomes?
    Lets say JoELeEt has had a dialup connection his whole life, and so has had the
    relative freedom of security that a national ISP provides him. Suddenly he comes
    online, and joins all his regular channels, and appears as:
    joe@pr666135-a.rct1.on.wave.home.com
    Well.. Now i know that JoELeEt lives in ontario, canada, and he is a happy
    subscriber to the @home system. Good for you, joe!
    But unfortunately, since i am a federal agent, i am going to be paying a visit to
    joe and his family, and i'm going to take joe away for a long time... ;/
    Bad for you, joe!

    People, i can't stress how important is is that you GET A BOUNCE. it doesn't have to
    be an eleet riches style spoof, (mray@i.own.you), it just has to be something that
    is NOT your home IP.
    DON'T get your home IP added on sites.. DO NOT LEECH from now... how many of you
    100% know who has adduser access on EVERY SINGLE site that you are on? all it takes
    is "site user ndetroit", and anyone with staff access on all my sites will get to
    see the IP of my shells and my home connection.
    What if just 1 of those people was a fed, who was trying to bust ndetroit?
    Heh... well... i guess today was his lucky day, wasn't it?

8.) Have a kill button
    Most good hackers that i know (and quite a few scene ppl) have a magic file called 
    "fun.exe" or something to that effect that will erase all the files that they
    specify within a matter of minutes. You should get a program like this, and you 
    should TEST it.. better to know that it works.
    The file should erase the data, and then overwrite it 7 times, or whatever the 
    lastest DOD standard is.. (no, the OTHER dod). 
    In addition, if your program is supposed to erase c:\sceneshit, then for fuck's sake,
    don't deviate from that directory, and start to store shit on your desktop.. ;p
    While we are on the subject, please dont' leave VCD's/ISO's/burned CD's lying
    around... store them somewhere non-obvious. It might be a bitch to have to go
    fish a CD out of the lampshape every time you need it, but.. in the long right you'll
    be better off.. ;p

9.) Shut the fuck up
    When you get busted, you are read your rights for a reason. Anything that you say
    can, and WILL be used against you in a court of law. It WILL... many times the
    police will arrest someone, and then IMPLY that if that person co-operates NOW,
    then things will be a lot easier on them in the long run. That's bullshit.
    You don't have to co-operate. In fact, you don't have to say a damn thing, except
    "I would like to call my lawyer now, please".
    For god sakes, don't start yelling "this is a conspiracy by THE MAN to take me down!!
    Well.. the scene will live on, man!!!! fuck you, i won't do what you tell me!!
    Razor1911 forevah!!!"... ... that would be stupid.
    Don't say a damn thing without your lawyer present. If the cops wanna cut a deal with
    you, (ie: "Give us the names of the leaders, suppliers, and crackers of the scene's
    big 3 groups, and we'll knock a couple years off your sentence, Mr. Lennon") then
    by all means cut a deal with them, AFTER your lawyer has shown up to preside
    over the whole business.
